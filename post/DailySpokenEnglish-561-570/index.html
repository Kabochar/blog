DailySpokenEnglish 561 570

2020年5月7日
| 阅读

第 57 / 70 篇…

1. Lean on me. 依靠我，依靠某人.

A: Ugh~ I’m so nervous. Moving to another country..…

B: Well，lean on me.I’m there for you.

A: Oh，I really appreciate it.

B: Hey! That’s what friends are for.

2. Sound asleep. Sleep very deeply.

A: Where were you last night?

B: I went to bed early. I was tired.

A: I called you three times!

B: Sorry! I was sound asleep.

3. Lay it on me! 空闲时，可以打扰我了。算在我头上

A: Do you have time now?

B: Oh，right. Your new idea. Lay it on me!

A: Well，I think you should make a better website.

B: Ugh~ I know! It’s urgent~~

4. Under my belt. 已有的经验

A: I’m a beginner，but can I join DDM?

B: Well，once you get my Daily Dictation videos under your belt, then you can join.

A: How much are those lessons?

B: How much? They’ re free!

5. Pull your weight. 滥竽充数，尽职尽责

A: Oh, no! ls John on our team?

B: Yeah，why?

A: He never pulls his weight.

B: Really? He seems like a hard worker.

6. Flickering. 闪烁

A: Do you have a minute?

B: What do you need?

A: The light in my room is flickering.

B: I’ll change it for you^^

7. Dork. 笨蛋

A: What are you doing?

B: Oh..… just… nothing.

A: Making faces in the mirror? Dork!

B: Leave me alone～

8. An urbanite. 都市人

A: Let’s go to Le Grange tonight^^

B: The restaurant? No thanks～

A: Why not?It sounds great-~

B: Pfft~ It’s for urbanites!

9. Farm-to-fork. 农场到餐桌

A: What are your grocery bills?

B: A little high. About $300.

A: Wow! Why so much?

B: I look for farm-to-fork foods. They’re pricier.

10. Rat’s nest. 老鼠我

A: I have to go to Eduardo’s today~

B: You don’t sound too excited.

A: No，his place is a rat’s nest. Yuck!

B: It can’t be that bad!
