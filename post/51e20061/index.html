    第 61 / 70 篇…

    1. The nitty-gritty. 细节

    A: I wish Coach Shane would teach more about pronunciation.

    B: He does in DDM and PIRF. He really gets into the nitty-gritty.

    A: But I don’t have any money.

    B: Investment in knowledge pays the best interest～

    2. A hollow victory. 空洞的胜利

    A: Germany killed Brazil in the World Cup.

    B: Yeah，but it was a hollow victory.

    A: How so? It was awesome!

    B: Yeah，but Brazil was without their number one player～

    3. Lock，stock and barrel. 完事就备

    A: Did you pack everything?

    B: Lock，stock and barrel!

    A: Great. Let’s go!

    B: Wait~Where are the keys?

    4. The lowdown. 低估

    A: Where can I get the lowdown on DDM Lite?

    B: He’s got a webpage: www. letsmasterenglish. com/ddmlite

    A: That page has all the info I need?

    B: Everything.

    5. Give him an inch and he’ll take a mile! 得寸进尺

    A: Shane，can I borrow your car this weekend?

    B: Okay. But don’t go too far.

    A: Don’t worry! Thanks^^ And please make sure the tank is full! Bye～~

    B: What?!! Give him an inch and he’ll take a mile!

    6. Watch your step! 小心台阶

    A: Let’s hurry!I don’t want to be late.

    B: Okay! Hey，watch your step-～

    A: Oh… gross!! Gum~～

    B: I told you to watch your step~

    7. To drop off .. Drop-off. 落下；下沉

    A: Drop me off over here.

    B: I dropped off Lucas here, too.

    A: It’s the best drop-off point for subway and bus users.

    B: I see. Watch your step as you get out–there’s quite a drop-off.

    8. Honest to a fault. 诚实得太过分

    A: Jim’s so honest. He just turned in a wallet full of cash!

    B: I hate Jim.

    A: What? He’s a great guy.

    B: Really? He told me yesterday I was gaining weight.

    A: Well… I guess he’s just honest to a fault!

    9. You’re smarter than you look! 你比看起来更聪明

    A: Why didn’t you tell your mom where you were?

    B: Because she would have worried too much.

    A: Wow! You’ re smarter than you look! Thanks, dad!

    B: Let’s enjoy the game^^

    10. To give credit where credit is due. 把功劳记在应该记的人或事物上.

    A: In British pronunciation they say “better”，but in American pronunciation they say"better”.

    B: Hey, your English pronunciation has really improved.

    A: Well，it’s thanks to you，Coach Shane.

    B: A little maybe，but let’s give credit where credit is due–you worked HARD!
