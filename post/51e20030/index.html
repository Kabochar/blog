第 30 天…

1.To crack open ~ 开一点点. To crack open the window.

A: Who’s smoking in there?

B: Me~

A: Crack open the window, dirty man.

B: Sorry-～

2.To feel stifled.

A: You look stressed.

B: Argh~I feel so stifled in this office.

A: Crack a window.

B: It’s not enough. I need trees!!!

3.To stifle something. To control something.

A: I’m leaving you.

B: Why?

A: You’ re stifling my emotions.

B: You can be emotional. But, not too loud. I’ve got a headache.

4.A crook. A cheater.

A: Is that my book?

B: Yeah.I’m just borrowing it.

A: Is that my pen?

B: Yeah,I needed that, too.

A: You’ re a crook.

5.Right off the bat. Immediately.

A: How was your English class?

B: Horrible.

A: Why?

B: The teacher gave us a test right off the bat～～

6.Food for thought. Here something you should do think about it.

A: I really want to master English.

B: Are you studying a lot?

A: Three days a week.

B: Here’s some food for thought: they say it takes10,000 hours to master something–how many hour shave you invested?

7.To scramble. 争抢. To hurry.

A: What are you doing, Sergej?

B: I’m getting ready for FNL.

A: Are you joining tonight?

B: Yeah, in a few minutes.

A: You’d better scramble. It fills up fast.

8.Out of sight, out of mind. 眼不见，心不烦

A: Are you okay since you broke up with Meg?

B: I’m okay-～Is that her picture on your wall?

A: Yeah..… it’s nice～~

B: BURN IT. Out of sight, out of mind.

9.It’ll never fly. It’ll never succeed.

A: So, you want to open a restaurant that sells..…

B: Bugs! They’ re so delicious.

A: It’ ll never fly.

B: Why not? Have you ever eaten one?

10.When pigs fly. 天方夜谭

A: Aren’t you studying for the math test?

B: I’m pretty confident.

A: But you skipped most of the classes.

B: I’ lI be fine.

A: Yeah, when pigs fly.
