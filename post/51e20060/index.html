    第 60 / 70 篇…

    1. To cut it close. 刚刚好

    A: There! Just in time.

    B: We have two minutes～

    A: Just in time.

    B: We cut it close. Let’s get here earlier tomorrow, please!

    2. Quite a few. 很少几个

    A: How many states have you visited?

    B: Quite a few. Maybe twenty.

    A: Which is your favorite?

    B: So far… Colorado was great!

    3. To kill two birds with one stone. 一石二鸟

    A: I wanna cook beef tonight.

    B: Look up a recipe with buttermilk.

    A: What? Why?

    B: This buttermilk is going to expire，so if you can use it in a beef recipe you’ll be killing two birds with one stone!

    4. To harbor a feeling of… 怀有 .. 的感觉

    A: Look at that guy～ Oh,I have a Porsche～～

    B: You seem to harbor a lot of hatred for rich people.

    A: No，just rich people who didn’t earn their money.

    B: How do you know how he got his money?

    5. Tit for tat. 针锋相对

    A: The US is angry at Russia so it wants to block Russian products.

    B: Russia might just do the same.

    A: Tit for tat～

    B: Kids

    6. To man the grill. To handle something.

    A: What’s that?

    B: Leftover hamburgers. They’ re from the barbecue last night.

    A: They look perfect! Who manned the grill?

    B: Shane，of course!

    7. I have a penchant for… 对什么热衷…

    A: What’s Shane doing?

    B: He’s making homemade caramel for his coffee!

    A: Wow!He made fudge brownies the other day～

    B: He has a penchant for sweet things!!

    8. To tie one on. Drink a lot.

    A: You look horrible.

    B: Well,I feel horrible.

    A: You tied one on last night，didn’t you?

    B: Oh yeah..…

    9. I was conned into VERBing… 被欺骗

    A: Ten dollars to park?

    B: Yeah，I was conned into paying it.

    A: How so?

    B: The guy told me there was no other place to park without getting a ticket. He totally lied to me.

    10. A sub/ to sub for… 替身

    A: I’m heading to the mountains tomorrow!

    B: But what about your classes?

    A: I’m getting a sub.

    B: Can I do it? Please?!
