DailySpokenEnglish 401 410

2020年5月7日
| 阅读

第 40 天…

1.To have no say. 没有发言权

A: I’m going to major in Philosophy.

B: What? Did you ask your parents?

A: They have no say.

B: Aren’t they paying for your education?

A: Yeah. So?

2.Rub my nose in it. You make a mistake.

A: I heard Eric broke up with you.

B: Yeah.

A: I heard he has a new girlfriend.

B: Uh-huh.

A: I heard she’s really rich.

B: Quit rubbing my nose in it!

3.Never delivers! 口是心非

A: Are you going to vote?

B: What for?

A: Politicians never deliver.

B: They never keep their promises, do they?

A: They’ re almost all the same~

4.Pick your brain. 听取某人的建议意见

A: Can I pick your brain?

B: Sure. What about?

A: American pronunciation.

B:’ That’s my specialty!

5.To call dibs. 有权做…

A: Hey, do you guys wanna go to the lake?

B: Yeah!!

A: Yeah! Dibs on the front seat.

B: Aw~You always get the front seat.

A: You need to call dibs!

6.To haul ass. Do it quickly.

A: Isn’t your meeting at 3?

B: Oh, no.I’m gonna be late.

A: You’d better haul ass.

B: Yep. See ya~~

7.Sit tight. Sit down but don’t move.

A: I sent you my application.

B: Now what should I do?

A: Just sit tight.

B: You’ll contact me?

A: Yes, we’ll contact you within two weeks.

8.No less (so) than. 不低于

A: I’m trying to sleep less these days.

B: Why? Sleep is important. No less so than food and water.

A: But I wanna start exercising.

B: Great, but don’t reduce your sleep.

9.Per se. 本身

A: Should I throw this away?

B: Don’t touch that t-shirt.

A: Why not? It’s not worth anything! It’s a rag!

B: It may not be valuable per se, but it means a lot to me.

10.Keep your nose clean. 保持检点

A: Where’s the seminar?

B: In Las Vegas!

A: That’s Sin City!

B: Yeah!

A: Keep your nose clean or I’ll kill you!
